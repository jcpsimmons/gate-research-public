### Section 1: What People Remember (~4 min)

- A conspiracy theory is going viral on TikTok and Reddit from adults who went through GATE in the 80s and 90s. The usual reaction is to dismiss it. But when you read through thousands of these accounts, a pattern emerges that's harder to wave away, because specific, unusual details repeat across people who have no connection to each other ([Yahoo News, Jan 2025](https://news.yahoo.com/enrolled-gifted-classes-child-now-230027333.html); [Reddit r/Gifted](https://www.reddit.com/r/Gifted/comments/1nq83q6/why_are_people_saying_so_many_strange_things/))
- The shared memories cluster around a set of exercises that don't look like normal gifted education: sitting in a dark room with heavy headphones listening to tones, being asked to guess symbols on cards (circle, plus sign, three wavy lines, square, star), describing images sealed inside manila envelopes, guided meditation with eye masks, and being asked to visualize and report what they "see." Consistently: windows covered with brown paper, sessions in a trailer or portable classroom, and an instructor whose name nobody can remember despite spending hours with them

- Other consistent reports: a pink bubblegum-flavored drink administered in paper cups, personality assessments, speed reading programs with SRA cards, an emphasis on ancient mythology (especially Egypt), lateral thinking games, and the feeling of being studied rather than taught. Three students arranged in specific roles: one "viewing," one monitoring, one designated as a target. These details repeat across people in different states, different decades, different school districts

Because those specific exercises have names. They're documented. And they trace back to places that have nothing to do with elementary school

### Section 2: The Exercises Have Names (~4 min)

- Start with the symbol cards. Circle, plus, wavy lines, square, star. Those are Zener cards, designed by Karl Zener in the 1930s for J.B. Rhine's parapsychology lab at Duke University. Rhine used them to test for extrasensory perception. His protocol: guess the card the viewer is holding, predict the next card in a sequence. The CIA has documents on Rhine's work in the FOIA Reading Room ([Wikipedia - Zener cards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zener_cards); [CIA Reading Room](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/nsa-rdp96x00790r000100040002-2))
- The three-person setup: one person views, one monitors, one is the target. That's the exact structure of the CIA's remote viewing protocol. A declassified CIA training document states it explicitly: "Remote viewing requires a viewer, a monitor and a target" ([CIA-RDP96-00789R002200070001-0](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/docs/CIA-RDP96-00789R002200070001-0.pdf)). The SRI protocol describes the viewer reporting impressions while the monitor records them and the target person is at a remote location. People are describing this as a childhood exercise
- Describing an image sealed inside a manila envelope. That is coordinate remote viewing. The standard SRI/Stargate protocol involved sealed envelopes containing target coordinates or images. The viewer would describe impressions of the target without opening the envelope. Harold Puthoff and Russell Targ's published methodology describes exactly this ([CIA-RDP96-00787R000500400001-4](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/cia-rdp96-00787r000500400001-4))
- Heavy headphones, guided meditation in a dark room, asked to visualize. That maps to Bob Monroe's Gateway Process. The Army/CIA's own 1983 assessment of Monroe's technique describes using binaural audio through headphones to induce altered states of consciousness, with the subject in a controlled environment reporting what they perceive. That assessment is in the CIA Reading Room ([CIA-RDP96-00788R001700210016-5](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/cia-rdp96-00788r001700210016-5))
- The personality tests. Here's where it gets specific. John W. Gittinger was a CIA psychologist who developed the Personality Assessment System (PAS). The PAS derives personality profiles from Wechsler Intelligence Scale subtest scores. That's the same IQ test administered to identify GATE children. A 1986 CIA document describes using the PAS to screen and select candidates for remote viewing training, and it also cross-references the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator ([CIA-RDP96-00789R002200010001-6](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/docs/CIA-RDP96-00789R002200010001-6.pdf)). Gittinger's work was part of MKUltra. He testified at the Senate hearings as a CIA psychologist ([Wikipedia - PAS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personality_Assessment_System))

- I'm not saying your third-grade teacher was CIA. I'm saying the exercises people describe match documented CIA research protocols with enough specificity that it's worth asking how they got into elementary school classrooms. ### Section 3: Sidney Marland: The Man Who Defined "Gifted" (~3 min)

- The first name on the resume stack is Sidney P. Marland Jr. Nixon nominated him as U.S. Commissioner of Education in 1970. Two years later, he published the Marland Report, the first time the federal government defined "giftedness" and told Congress to start systematically identifying these kids nationwide ([Marland Report PDF](https://www.valdosta.edu/colleges/education/human-services/document%20/marland-report.pdf); [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marland_report))
- Before any of that, Marland was in the Pacific in WWII. Colonel, 43rd Infantry Division. His family biography says he "directed military intelligence research" ([Marland Family UK](https://marland-family.uk/people/sidney_marland.htm)). That's the claim that floats around online, and it sounds damning
- But I actually checked the primary sources. Two independent Army records identify his staff role as G-3, which is Operations, not G-2, which is Intelligence ([Archive.org](http://Archive.org); [Texas A&M TARO](https://txarchives.org/tamucush/finding_aids/00077.xml)). The "directed military intelligence research" claim doesn't hold against the Army's own paperwork. That said, G-3 and G-2 work hand-in-glove at the divisional staff level. And his DSC citation lists him under "Chief of Staff, 43rd Infantry Division," meaning he was coordinating across the entire general staff, including intelligence ([Home of Heroes](https://homeofheroes.com/recipient/sidney-p-marland-jr/); [Defense.gov](http://Defense.gov) [DSC list](https://valor.defense.gov/portals/24/documents/servicecross/armydsc-wwii.pdf))
- After leaving government, he became president of the College Board from 1973 to 1978 ([LA Times Obituary](https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1992-06-03-mn-517-story.html)). So the man who told the country which children count as "gifted" then went to run the test that sorts them for college admissions
- Honest framing: decorated combat officer who moved through Cold War policy networks into education. Not a spy. But the pipeline from senior Army staff to "let's build a federal system to identify the most exceptional children" deserves examination

### Section 4: Harold Lyon: From Recondo School to the Office of Gifted and Talented (~5 min)

- If Marland wrote the blueprint, Harold Lyon built the house. He was the first Director of the Office of Gifted and Talented in the U.S. Department of Education. Eight years. He co-wrote the white paper for the Jacob Javits Gifted and Talented Students Education Act ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harold_C._Lyon_Jr.); [NYT 1980](https://www.nytimes.com/1980/07/29/archives/programs-for-gifted-schoolchildren-get-increasing-attention-from.html))
- His resume before that: West Point Class of 1958, appointed by then-Senator John F. Kennedy. Ranger-qualified, Airborne-qualified, 101st Airborne Division. At Fort Campbell, he established the Army's first Recondo School: Reconnaissance-Commando. Curriculum: intelligence gathering, infiltration, enemy vehicle recognition, resistance to interrogation, sabotage ([West Point Alumni](http://www.west-point.org/users/usma1958/21905/); [Obituary](https://www.laconiadailysun.com/community/obituaries/dr-harold-clifford-hal-lyon-jr-84/article_aadc8968-0b08-11ea-88be-4b036b54ef39.html); [Wikipedia Recondo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recondo))
- So the man who founded a school to train soldiers in reconnaissance, infiltration, and intelligence collection then pivoted to founding the federal office that identifies gifted children. Keep that transition in mind
- Before the gifted office, he was the federal project officer for Sesame Street, working through foundation networks (Carnegie, Ford) with documented CIA-aligned Cold War activities. Ford funded the Congress for Cultural Freedom, exposed in 1967 as a CIA front ([Issue Lab](https://www.issuelab.org/resource/a-most-interesting-and-complex-involvement-cold-war-alignments-between-the-ford-and-rockefeller-foundations-the-congress-for-cultural-freedom-and-the-central-intelligence-agency.html)). Lyon was operating inside these networks before he ever touched gifted education
- The connection that ties it together: Lyon "became friends with George Leonard, Michael Murphy, many of whom he invited into Washington to give seminars" to Congressional staff ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harold_C._Lyon_Jr.)). Michael Murphy co-founded Esalen Institute. George Leonard, Esalen's de facto third founder, was "an analytical intelligence officer" during the Korean War ([Esalen In Memoriam](https://www.esalen.org/in-memoriam/george-leonard)). The man running federal gifted education was personally connected to Esalen's founders, one of whom was a former intelligence officer, and he brought them into Washington
- Unconfirmed thread worth flagging: a Lt. Col. Harold C. Lyon wrote a 1948 Army paper on T-Force intelligence operations during the liberation of Paris. Lyon Jr.'s father was described as "an Airborne Infantry officer." Same name, same branch ([Army Donovan Papers](https://mcoecbamcoepwprd01.blob.core.usgovcloudapi.net/library/DonovanPapers/wwii/STUP2/G-L/LyonHaroldC%20LTC.pdf))
- Personal angle: "As someone who spent years at Anduril, I can tell you: the line between defense, intelligence, and civilian technology is not a line. It's a revolving door. Seeing the same pattern in 1970s education policy is not surprising. It's just underdocumented."

### Section 5: Stanley Krippner: The Dream Lab and the CIA's Files (~4 min)

- This is the strongest single thread, because it's one person occupying two worlds simultaneously with the paperwork to prove it
- Stanley Krippner published five papers in the Gifted Child Quarterly between 1961 and 1965, including "Creativity and Psychic Phenomena" (1963). He was listed as a contributor to California's Mentally Gifted Minor program, the direct precursor to GATE ([Sage Journals](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/001698626300700202); [Google Groups MGM](https://groups.google.com/g/mentally-gifted-minors-california1/c/INBrGBu22BM))
- At the exact same time, from 1964 to 1973, he directed the Maimonides Dream Laboratory in Brooklyn, doing dream telepathy experiments with EEG-monitored subjects ([Psi Encyclopedia](https://psi-encyclopedia.spr.ac.uk/articles/maimonides-dream-telepathy-research/)). In 1967, he published "Characteristics of Gifted and Talented Youth" from his Maimonides Medical Center address. Same institution, same desk ([ERIC](https://eric.ed.gov/?id=ED015503))
- His work appears in at least six CIA Reading Room documents, all in the STAR GATE/parapsychology collection. A 1991 SAIC document cites Maimonides and Krippner as foundational precedent for the entire remote viewing program ([CIA-RDP96-00789R003100140001-2](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/cia-rdp96-00789r003100140001-2)). Other documents track his visit to China to investigate psychic children ([CIA-RDP96-00791R000200230044-4](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/docs/CIA-RDP96-00791R000200230044-4.pdf))
- The Grateful Dead experiment: February 1971. Jerry Garcia personally proposed it. 2,000-plus audience members as "telepathic senders" while a subject slept at Maimonides with EEG monitoring. This happened. It's documented. It's in CIA files ([Krippner's account](https://stanleykrippner.weebly.com/a-pilot-study-in-dream-telepathy-with-the-grateful-dead.html))
- No direct CIA funding to Krippner has been documented. He built the civilian science, the intelligence community treated it as the foundation for their classified programs. But when the same researcher is also shaping gifted education policy, the overlap is hard to ignore

### Section 6: The Bigger Web: MKUltra, Esalen, and Stargate (~5 min)

- Everything so far is about people. Now zoom out to the programs, because the intelligence community wasn't just passively watching. They were running their own parallel efforts on consciousness, behavior, and human potential
- MKUltra, 1953 to 1973, 149-plus subprojects. They used children. Subproject 47 tested drugs including LSD on juveniles at Bordentown, NJ ([CIA FOIA](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/00017445); [NSA GWU](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/dnsa-intelligence/2024-12-23/cia-behavior-control-experiments-focus-new-scholarly)). More relevant: Subproject 112, University of Indiana, "Children's Conceptions of Occupational Roles," CIA interest in "application to selection of technical and scientific careers." The CIA was funding research on how to steer children toward intelligence-relevant work. That's not a conspiracy theory; it's a line item
- Esalen: Lyon's friend Murphy co-founded it. Gregory Bateson, OSS veteran, lived there until he died in 1980 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gregory_Bateson)). A CIA FOIA doc from 1983 quotes U.S. officials: "Some believe the Kremlin might have special uses for the human-potential movement... techniques of behavior modification, or mind control" ([CIA-RDP90-00806R000100380026-1](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/cia-rdp90-00806r000100380026-1)). CIA wasn't just aware of Esalen. They were tracking it
- Same "human potential" language shows up in the Stargate Project, DIA, 1977-1995. Dale Graff coined the name "to represent an innovative effort for expanding human potential" ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stargate_Project_(U.S._Army_unit))). One CIA document uses "gifted individuals" to describe the remote viewers they were recruiting ([CIA-RDP96-00787R000200210003-6](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/docs/CIA-RDP96-00787R000200210003-6.pdf))
- Skip Atwater, Stargate's operations officer for a decade, later became president of the Monroe Institute. Remember the Gateway Process headphones? Same lineage ([CIA-RDP96-00788R001700210016-5](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/cia-rdp96-00788r001700210016-5))
- The Duke connection. GATE kids in multiple states report being invited to summer programs at Duke University through the Talent Identification Program. Duke is also the institution where J.B. Rhine ran his parapsychology lab using the same Zener cards people remember from GATE classrooms. Rhine's lab operated there from 1935 to 1965. Duke TIP launched in 1980 ([Duke TIP archives](https://archives.lib.duke.edu/catalog/uatip); [Wikipedia - Duke TIP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Talent_Identification_Program)). I'm not claiming Duke TIP was a parapsychology screening program. I'm saying that the same institution that housed the foundational ESP research also built the pipeline for gifted children, and the tools overlap
- And then there's E. Paul Torrance, the man who created the creativity tests used across GATE programs. Before he developed the Torrance Tests of Creative Thinking, he spent seven years as a research psychologist for the Air Force Survival Training Program. His description of a creative person was identical to his description of a fighter jet ace: "a courageous risk-taker who is independent and committed" ([NAGC](https://www.nagc.org/news/the-father-of-creativity-the-life-legacy-and-lessons-of-dr-e-paul-torrance-1915---2003)). The creativity test in your GATE classroom was designed by a military psychologist who explicitly saw creative ability as a national security asset
- January 2025: Trump announced a $500 billion AI venture called Stargate. OpenAI, SoftBank, Oracle. They say it's named after the 1994 film. Dale Graff named the original after "expanding human potential." Same word. Different substrate. Maybe coincidence. Maybe not

### Section 7: What This Actually Means (~3 min)

- Let me be precise about what I'm not saying. No declassified document puts CIA operatives inside GATE classrooms. No document shows children were screened for psychic abilities. No document links Stargate recruitment to K-12 gifted education. The 1995 AIR evaluation found zero actionable intelligence from 20 years of remote viewing ([CIA AIR Evaluation](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/cia-rdp96-00791r000200180005-5))
- What I am saying: the same networks of people interested in consciousness research, psychic phenomena, and behavioral modification for intelligence purposes were also the people building the national infrastructure to identify and sort cognitively exceptional children. Marland came from Army operations. Lyon came from the Recondo School and befriended Esalen's founders. Krippner straddled gifted ed and CIA-filed parapsychology from the same desk. MKUltra's Subproject 112 explicitly studied steering children into intelligence careers. The Wechsler IQ test that identified GATE kids is the same test a CIA psychologist used to profile remote viewing candidates. The Zener cards, the three-person protocol, the envelope exercises, the Gateway-style headphones: these aren't fever dreams. They're documented research tools with CIA file numbers
- The question isn't "was GATE a CIA program." That framing is too simple, and it lets everyone off the hook. The real question is: when the federal government builds a system to identify the most cognitively exceptional children in the country, and the people building it are embedded in intelligence community networks, and the exercises used in those classrooms match documented CIA research protocols, what does that tell you about how power actually works?
